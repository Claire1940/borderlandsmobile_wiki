## Playable Characters and Skill Trees

Currently, the game features a single playable character: a powerful Siren whose name is still being finalized in the early build. Unlike previous entries where players chose from four distinct Vault Hunters, this mobile title focuses on a deep, singular progression path.

The Siren’s primary gimmick is her companion, a Skag named **Nigel**. Nigel functions similarly to FL4K’s pets in Borderlands 3, roaming the battlefield and automatically engaging enemies. This makes the game more accessible for touch-screen users, as the pet provides consistent damage output even when the player is repositioning.

### Skill Tree Simplicity
The skill tree in this mobile iteration is streamlined compared to the mainline console versions. Instead of complex branching paths that fundamentally change gameplay, the skills focus on core stat boosts:
*   **Action Skill Augments:** Enhancing Nigel's damage or the Siren's elemental reach.
*   **Passive Buffs:** Increases to reload speed, fire rate, and maximum health.
*   **Utility:** Cooldown reductions for the Siren’s signature abilities.

## Loot Systems and Gear Progression

The heart of any Borderlands game is the loot, and the mobile version introduces several new mechanics to facilitate the "forever grind" on a smaller screen. The rarity tiers remain familiar, ranging from Common (White) to Legendary (Orange).

### The Loot Crate Mechanic
Instead of relying solely on boss drops, players can access various loot crates from the main menu hub. These crates provide a steady stream of gear to ensure players never hit a progression wall.

| Crate Tier | Acquisition Method | Cooldown/Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Common** | Time-based free claim | 10 Minutes |
| **Rare** | Challenge Keys | Gameplay Rewards |
| **Epic** | Premium Keys / Hard Challenges | Weekly Missions |
| **Legendary** | Achievement Rewards | End-game Content |

### Scrap and Upgrading
A significant departure from the mainline games is the **Scrap Gear** system. Players can dismantle unwanted weapons to receive Scrap, which is then used to upgrade the stats of their favorite guns, grenades, and shields. This system mirrors the "Guardian Rank" or "Badass Rank" from previous games but applies directly to individual pieces of equipment.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when scrapping gear! Once a weapon is turned into scrap, it cannot be recovered. Always lock your favorite Legendaries before cleaning out your inventory.

## Mobile-Optimized Mechanics

To accommodate the platform, several "quality of life" features have been implemented. These are designed to reduce the friction of touch-screen controls while maintaining the series' identity.

*   **Auto-Loot:** Money, ammo, and gear are automatically picked up when the player walks over them.
*   **Auto-Sensing Containers:** Running near a crate or locker will automatically trigger it to open and scavenge the contents.
*   **Auto-Shoot:** A toggleable feature that fires the weapon automatically when the reticle is over an enemy.
*   **Vaughn’s AFK Rewards:** A unique feature where the NPC Vaughn collects experience and currency over time, allowing players to progress even when they aren't actively playing.

## Weapon Customization: Mods

Beyond the basic upgrade system, players can find and equip **Weapon Mods**. These function as attachments that provide specific bonuses to a gun's performance. For example, a fire rate mod can be equipped to an assault rifle to increase its DPS, while a reload mod might be better suited for a high-damage shotgun. These mods can also be leveled up independently, adding another layer to the game's deep customization.

| Mod Type | Primary Benefit | Best Paired With |
