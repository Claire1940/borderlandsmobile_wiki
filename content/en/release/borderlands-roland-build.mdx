## The Core of the Soldier: Skill Tree Priorities

Roland’s skill trees—Infantry, Support, and Medic—offer a distinct path for every playstyle. While later games in the series introduced complex skill interactions, Borderlands 1 focuses on raw stat boosts and utility. For a standard **borderlands roland build**, you generally want to prioritize damage and cooldown reduction first.

### Infantry Tree: Maximizing DPS
The Infantry tree is where Roland finds his killing power. Skills like **Impact** provide a flat damage increase to all weapon types, making it a mandatory 5/5 investment for any serious player. **Metal Storm** is perhaps his most iconic skill, granting a massive fire rate boost and recoil reduction immediately after a kill.

| Skill | Type | Effect at 5/5 | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | Passive | +15% Bullet Damage | **High** |
| **Metal Storm** | Kill Skill | +60% Fire Rate / -75% Recoil | **High** |
| **Scattershot** | Passive | +25% Shotgun Damage / -25% Spread | Medium |
| **Refire** | Passive | Reduces Cooldown on Hit | **High** |

> **💡 Tip:** Even if you aren't a shotgun specialist, putting points into Scattershot can make legendary shotguns like the "Commando" or "Crux" absolutely devastating at mid-range.

### Support Tree: Resource Management
The Support tree ensures that your **borderlands roland build** never runs out of steam. **Defense** and **Quick Charge** are vital for shield sustain, while **Deploy** is necessary to keep your Scorpio Turret active as often as possible.

| Skill | Type | Effect at 5/5 | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Defense** | Passive | +25% Shield Recharge Rate | Medium |
| **Quick Charge** | Kill Skill | Regenerates 7% Shield / sec | **High** |
| **Deploy** | Passive | -100% Turret Cooldown Time | **High** |
| **Supply Drop** | Turret | Periodic Ammo/Grenade Drops | Medium |

## The Best Solo Borderlands Roland Build

When playing solo in 2026, survival is just as important as damage. A solo-focused **borderlands roland build** emphasizes health regeneration and shield recovery. By combining the Infantry and Medic trees, you can create a "tank" setup that heals while dealing damage.

1.  **Level 5-15**: Focus on **Impact** and **Defense**. This provides a solid baseline of damage and survivability.
2.  **Level 16-25**: Max out **Metal Storm** and **Quick Charge**. These two kill skills create a loop where every kill makes you faster and harder to kill.
3.  **Level 26-35**: Invest in the Medic tree, specifically **Fitness** for extra health and **Overload** for increased magazine size.
4.  **Endgame (Level 50-69)**: Reach the capstone **Stat** in the Medic tree. This skill provides health regeneration to you and nearby allies whenever you kill an enemy, which is crucial for solo boss fights.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid the **Guided Missile** capstone if you prefer precision. While the rockets add extra damage, they can occasionally miss or knock enemies out of your line of fire.

## Essential Gear and Class Mods

A **borderlands roland build** is only as good as the Class Mod (COM) supporting it. Roland has some of the most specialized COMs in the game, allowing him to focus on specific weapon manufacturers or team roles.

### Top Tier Class Mods

*   **Heavy Gunner**: This is widely considered the best overall COM for Roland. It increases magazine size and fire rate, which synergizes perfectly with high-capacity Combat Rifles.
*   **Commando**: If you prefer shotguns, the Commando mod provides massive boosts to shotgun accuracy and damage, turning them into sniper-hybrids.
*   **Tactician**: Ideal for players who want their turret up 100% of the time. It boosts shield recharge and reduces action skill cooldown.
*   **Leader**: Perfect for power-leveling friends, as it increases experience gained for the entire party.

### Recommended Weaponry
For your primary slots, look for weapons with high fire rates to proc **Refire** more frequently. The **Ogre** (Atlas Combat Rifle) and the **Hellfire** (Maliwan SMG) are legendary choices that benefit immensely from Roland's skills. For shotguns, a **Matador** or **Bulldog** with high pellet counts will maximize the benefits of the Scattershot skill.

| Weapon Type | Preferred Prefix | Key Synergy |
|---|---|---|
| **Combat Rifle** | Ravenous / Ogre | Metal Storm / Overload |
| **Shotgun** | Terrible / Matador | Scattershot / Impact |
| **SMG** | Double / Hellfire | Refire / Quick Charge |

## Advanced Co-op Strategies

In a team environment, Roland transitions into a combat medic. The **borderlands roland build** for co-op should always include **Cauterize**, which allows you to heal teammates by shooting them. This might seem counterintuitive, but in high-level play, it is often the only way to keep a Berserker or Siren alive during intense firefights.

**Revive** is another essential co-op skill. It gives your Scorpio Turret a chance to instantly revive downed allies within its radius. When paired with **Deploy** and **Refire**, you can throw your turret out multiple times in a single fight, acting as a persistent safety net for your squad.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon for a Borderlands Roland build?**

A: While Roland is proficient with many weapons, Combat Rifles (especially those with the "Ogre" or "Destroyer" prefix) and high-capacity Shotguns are generally the best. These weapons benefit the most from his magazine size and fire rate buffs.

**Q: Does Roland's turret scale well into the endgame?**

A: In the late game (Level 61-69), the turret's damage falls off slightly compared to the player's weapons. However, its utility—providing cover, ammo regeneration via **Supply Drop**, and health via **Aid Station**—remains top-tier throughout the entire game.

**Q: Can I play Roland as a sniper?**

A: While you can use snipers, Roland lacks the specific critical hit and accuracy buffs found in Mordecai's skill trees. Roland is much more effective at mid-to-close range where his fire rate and shotgun skills can shine.

**Q: How do I reduce the Scorpio Turret cooldown?**

A: To maximize turret uptime, you should invest 5 points into **Deploy** (Support Tree) and **Refire** (Infantry Tree). Additionally, look for a "Tactician" or "Commando" class mod that offers a "+ Cooldown Reduction" stat.
