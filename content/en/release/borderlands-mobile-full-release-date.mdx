## Gameplay Mechanics and Character Progression

The mobile title retains the core "shoot and loot" loop that defined the franchise. Players currently have access to a single playable character: an unnamed Siren. This Siren features a unique gimmick where she can summon a loyal Scagg named **Nigel**. Nigel functions similarly to FL4K’s pets in Borderlands 3, attacking enemies and drawing aggro while the player focuses on gunplay.

### Skill Trees and Customization
The skill tree in this mobile iteration is streamlined compared to the mainline games. Instead of complex branching paths that fundamentally change how a character plays, the current trees focus on incremental stat boosts.

*   **Action Skills:** One primary active ability per character.
*   **Passive Boosts:** Increased reload speed, higher fire rates, and HP bonuses.
*   **Simple Stats:** The focus is on raw power rather than complex synergies.

### The Scrap System
One of the most significant departures from the console games is the introduction of the **Scrap System**. Instead of simply selling unwanted gear to a vendor, players can "scrap" items to earn resources used for permanent upgrades.

| Upgrade Category | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Gun Upgrades** | Increases base damage and handling for specific weapon types. |
| **Shield Upgrades** | Enhances capacity and reduces recharge delay. |
| **Grenade Mods** | Boosts blast radius and elemental damage. |
| **Weapon Mods** | Adds attachments like fire rate or reload speed buffs. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always scrap your white and green tier gear early on. The stat bonuses from the Scrap System are essential for surviving the higher-difficulty mobbing waves in the Tower of Terror.

## Game Modes and Endgame Content

Despite being a mobile title, the game features a surprising amount of content right out of the gate. The hub area is **Sanctuary 3**, where players can interact with familiar faces and access various mission types. While the story missions serve as a prologue, the real meat of the game lies in its repeatable encounters.

### Mission Varieties
1.  **Campaign Missions:** Linear story-driven quests that introduce the world and mechanics.
2.  **Tower of Terror:** A mobbing-focused mode where players collect ship parts under pressure.
3.  **Circle of Slaughter:** Wave-based survival against increasingly difficult enemies.
4.  **Psycho Lance:** A high-level mode featuring enemy modifiers that change gameplay dynamics.
5.  **Bounties:** Specific boss-killing missions that offer guaranteed high-tier rewards.
6.  **Raid Bosses:** Massive encounters, such as the Valkyrie Squad, designed for endgame progression.

### Faction Wars and Social Features
The game also introduces **Faction Wars**, where players can compete or cooperate against different in-game groups. While the social features are still being polished, the presence of a Battle Pass (including a free and upcoming "Ultimate" tier) indicates a long-term live-service model.

## Monetization and Loot Boxes

As a free-to-play title, the game utilizes a loot box system for gear acquisition. These crates are separated into tiers: Common, Rare, Epic, and Legendary.

*   **Common Crates:** These have a 10-minute cooldown and provide basic gear for free.
*   **High-Tier Crates:** These require keys, which are earned by completing daily and weekly challenges.
*   **Battle Pass:** Provides a steady stream of resources, skins, and keys.
