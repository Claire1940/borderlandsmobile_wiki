## Gear Customization and the Scrap System

To truly optimize the **borderlands mobile best class**, you must master the gear modification system. Unlike the mainline games where you simply find a better gun, Borderlands Mobile encourages you to "Scrap" unwanted items to fuel the growth of your current loadout. This creates a vertical progression system where even a "Green" rarity weapon can become a powerhouse if upgraded sufficiently.

### Weapon Mods
On the right side of your inventory, you will find slots for Gun Mods. These are collectible attachments that provide specific buffs to your weaponry. For example, slapping a Fire Rate mod on a Jakobs-style shotgun can mitigate its slow speed, while a Reload mod on a Tediore-style weapon increases your overall explosive output.

*   **Fire Rate Mods**: Best for Assault Rifles and SMGs.
*   **Reload Mods**: Essential for Shotguns and Snipers.
*   **Elemental Mods**: Add Shock, Corrosive, or Incendiary damage to any base weapon.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when scrapping gear. Once an item is converted to Scrap, it cannot be recovered. Always lock your Legendary and Epic items to prevent accidental deletion.

## Optimizing Mobile Controls

Playing a high-speed shooter on a touchscreen can be challenging. To make the most of your class, it is highly recommended to dive into the settings and adjust the automation features. Borderlands Mobile includes an "Auto-Shoot" toggle that fires your weapon whenever your reticle passes over an enemy. While purists may prefer manual control, Auto-Shoot is incredibly effective for maintaining high DPS while moving and double-jumping.

Additionally, the game features "Auto-Loot," which automatically picks up ammo, currency, and health vials. This allows you to stay focused on the combat rather than scanning the floor for drops. If you find the touchscreen controls cumbersome, the game does offer full controller support, which is the preferred way to play for competitive Faction Wars.
